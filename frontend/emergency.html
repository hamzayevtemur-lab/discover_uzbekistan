What To Do In An Emergency
Step-by-step guide for common situations
🪪
Lost Passport
1. Call your embassy immediately (see list above)
2. File a report at the nearest police station (call 102)
3. Contact Tourist Police: 1050
4. Keep a digital copy of your passport in email/cloud at all times
💳
Stolen Wallet / Cards
1. Block your card immediately — call your bank's international number
2. File a police report: call 102
3. Contact your embassy for emergency funds if needed
4. Western Union / MoneyGram available in Tashkent
🏥
Medical Emergency
1. Call ambulance: 103
2. Tell them your location — use Google Maps pin
3. Keep your travel insurance info accessible
4. Most pharmacies (apteka) are open until 22:00
🚗
Road Accident
1. Call police: 102 — do not move vehicles
2. Ambulance: 103 if anyone is injured
3. Take photos of the scene and all vehicles
4. Get the other driver's ID and insurance details
🌡️
Heat & Dehydration
Uzbekistan gets very hot (40°C+) in summer.
Drink 2–3 litres of water daily.
Avoid being outside 12:00–16:00 in July–August.
If dizzy — find shade, drink water, call 103 if serious.
📶
No Internet / Lost
1. Ask locals — Uzbeks are very helpful to tourists
2. Download offline maps (Maps.me) before travelling
3. Most hotels have free WiFi
4. Buy a local SIM — Ucell, Beeline or UzMobile (~$3)
🙏
Stay Safe & Enjoy Uzbekistan
Uzbekistan is generally a very safe country for tourists.
The people are warm and hospitable — don't hesitate to ask locals for help.
